OUTLAWS & ICONS

Red Dead Redemption 2

Red Dead Redemption 2 (RDR2) is an open-world action-adventure game developed by Rockstar Games, set in 1899 during the decline of the American Wild West. The story follows Arthur Morgan, an outlaw and senior member of the Van der Linde gang, as he navigates a world of lawmen, bounty hunters, and internal gang conflicts.

As civilization encroaches on the frontier, Arthur grapples with loyalty, morality, and his own fading place in a rapidly changing world. The game is renowned for its immersive storytelling, breathtaking open-world realism, and deep character development. With dynamic gameplay, stunning landscapes, and an emotionally gripping narrative, RDR2 is considered one of the greatest video games of all time.

Arthur Morgan

The protagonist of RDR2, Arthur was Dutch Van der Linde’s most loyal enforcer. Raised in an outlaw gang, he followed Dutch until he started questioning his morality. After being diagnosed with tuberculosis, Arthur sought redemption for his violent past.

Fun Fact: Arthur has different journal entries depending on how players treat people in the game.

Dutch Van der Linde

The charismatic leader of the gang, Dutch dreamed of freedom but gradually became more unstable and ruthless. He believed in loyalty, but his paranoia led to betrayal and destruction.

Fun Fact: His famous line, “Have some goddamn faith,” became a meme among fans.

John Marston

The protagonist of RDR1, John was a former outlaw trying to build a normal life for his wife and son. He was forced to hunt down his old gang to secure their freedom. His tragic fate sealed his status as one of gaming’s most beloved characters.

Fun Fact: John's scars were given to him by a wolf when he was younger.

Sadie Adler

Initially a widow whose husband was murdered by the O'Driscoll gang, Sadie evolves into one of the toughest, most ruthless members of the gang, seeking revenge.

Fun Fact: Sadie is one of the few characters in RDR2 whose arc ends with her achieving independence rather than tragedy.
